# Newpress Interpreter

A workspace for translating, editing, and arranging interview transcripts for documentary film.

## Register
product

## Users
Documentary filmmakers and the bilingual editors and translators they work with. They spend hours per session reading dense, multilingual transcripts, comparing original-language source against English translations, marking soundbites, refining phrasings, and exporting subtitle and selects-sequence files to Premiere Pro. Most sessions are 1–4 hours of focused close-reading and writing. Many users are over 35 and value a calm, editorial environment over a flashy dashboard.

## Product Purpose
Take a raw multilingual interview (CSV / JSON / Trint export) through five stages — upload, analyze, clarify, translate, edit — then export polished SRT subtitles, Premiere XML markers, selects sequences, and PDF highlight reels. Heavy AI assistance for translation, summarization, and soundbite hunting; the human editor is in charge throughout.

## Brand Voice
Editorial. Reverent of the source material. Self-aware about the work being craft, not content. The wordmark is "§ interpreter" — the section symbol stands in for "sacred section." Tagline: "where sacred sequences find their voice." Copy is plain, lowercase-friendly, never marketing-shouty. Italic Cormorant serif for headers and pull quotes; JetBrains Mono for labels, controls, and timecodes; Instrument Sans for actions. Loud feedback only for failures; success states are quiet.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS dashboards (Notion, Linear, Asana — too clinical, too cards-everywhere).
- "Modern" gradient-laden marketing-tool UIs (Canva, Figma plugins, AI writing apps).
- Skeuomorphic editor chrome (Adobe Premiere itself — too dark, too dense, too tool-jargon).
- Transcription-as-utility apps (Otter, Rev.com — they treat the text as data; we treat it as text).

## Strategic Principles
- **Editorial, not industrial.** This is a place to read. Big margins, real serif body type, comfortable line length, no chrome competing with the words.
- **Original + translation, side by side in the mind.** Even when only the English is shown, the layout should feel like a careful translation, not a generated output.
- **Trust the user.** Power features (Smart Sync, SOT Hunter, command palette, version history) are first-class, not hidden in menus.
- **Silence by default.** No animations on idle state. Color is held back. The interface gets out of the way of the writing.
- **Brand color is structural, not decorative.** Red `#DD2C1E` marks moments of intent — the speaker dot, a button, a pulled quote. Never as background or gradient.

## Aesthetic Anchors
- Warm cream paper background (`#F4F2E6`).
- Ink-near-black text (`#121118`).
- Burgundy / sepia for secondary text (`#412C27`).
- A single red accent (`#DD2C1E`) used sparingly.
- Type stack: Cormorant (serif), JetBrains Mono, Instrument Sans.
- Long paragraphs of body text in Cormorant at ~17–19 px, line-height ~1.55, max ~70 ch wide.
- Spacing scale based on 4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64 / 96 px.
